About Geckos

Gecko Species

There are over 2,000 species of geckos in the world. Some of the most common species include:

  • Leopard gecko (Eublepharis macularius)
  • Crested gecko (Rhacodactylus ciliatus)
  • Fat-tailed gecko (Hemitheconyx caudicinctus)
  • Tokay gecko (Gekko gecko)
  • House gecko (Hemidactylus frenatus)
  • Day gecko (Phelsuma spp.)

Diet

Geckos are insectivores, meaning they eat insects. They also eat other small animals, such as spiders, scorpions, and centipedes. Some species of geckos also eat fruits and vegetables.

Habitat

Geckos live in a variety of habitats, including forests, deserts, and even human homes. Some species of geckos are arboreal, meaning they live in trees. Other species of geckos are terrestrial, meaning they live on the ground.
